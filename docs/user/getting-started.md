# Getting started

Install the mod (see the [README](../../README.md#installation)), load
your world — existing saves are fine — and two things are different.

## Doors stop getting stuck open

You know the door: a switch or a key swings it open, and vanilla leaves
it *locked in the open position* — the Close prompt is there, but
pressing E just buzzes "locked" at you.

Now that door is simply unlocked. Walk through, press E, closed. Press
E again, open. Doors already stuck open in your save fix themselves as
their area loads, or the first time you press E on them.

## Locked doors can be picked

Look at a closed locked door with lockpicks in your inventory and press
E. Instead of the locked buzzer you get the same hold-to-pick timer as
a safe or a police car. When the timer finishes, the door is unlocked —
it stays closed until you open it.

- A wooden house door pops in seconds. A vault door takes half a minute
  and will probably eat a pick or two on the way.
- Picks can snap. Your progress is kept — the retry starts where the
  broken pick left off.
- No lockpicks? The E-press tells you what you're missing.

Picking makes noise, and noise has consequences — read
[Lockpicking](lockpicking.md) before you try it in a sleeper-filled
POI.

## What has NOT changed

- Your own base doors: nobody can close, unlock or pick a door you
  placed. That is all exactly vanilla.
- Trader gates: they lock for the night as always and cannot be picked.
- Locked doors never silently open — it is the pick minigame or the
  sledgehammer, your choice.
