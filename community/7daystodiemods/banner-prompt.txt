STFD banner - AI image generation prompt
====================================================

Target: 19:6 banner (e.g. 1900x600). Most generators don't offer 19:6
directly - generate at 21:9 and crop height, or generate 16:9 and
extend/crop. Match the StowIt banner style (M00-StowIt
docs/images/banner.png) so the M00 series reads as one family.

--- GUARDRAIL-SAFE PROMPT (use this for ChatGPT/DALL-E) ---

ChatGPT refuses the detailed prompt below: "lockpick + tension wrench +
stealthy + door" pattern-matches to real-world break-in instructions.
This variant keeps the scene and drops the trigger words - the crouched
pose and the glowing lock plate tell the story on their own.

Official key art for a fictional zombie-survival video game mod, in the
photorealistic gritty render style of 7 Days To Die. Ultra-wide banner
composition. A ruined building interior at night: rough wooden walls,
cracked plaster, debris on worn floorboards, cold blue moonlight through
a broken boarded window on the far left.

On the right third: a massive weathered vault-style metal door with
rusted rivets, a small barred viewing window, and a large ornate antique
lock plate. Center-right, seen from behind at a low three-quarter angle:
a lone survivor in ragged early-game clothing - worn t-shirt, patched
trousers, cloth hand wraps, small shabby backpack - crouched low on one
knee at the door, leaning close, hands raised to the old lock mechanism,
which GLOWS from within: warm amber-gold light and drifting ember
sparks pour from the lock plate around the survivor's hands, the only
warm light in a cold dark scene. Behind the door's barred window, deep
in shadow, the faint silhouette of a dormant zombie.

The left third is dark negative space with a giant distressed stencil
headline in cracked bone-white paint, all caps: "SHUT THE FRIGGING
DOOR", and beneath it smaller in the same style: "SHUT OPEN DOORS. PICK
LOCKED ONES. SPARE THE SLEDGE." Moody desaturated brown-and-charcoal
palette, one warm amber accent, soft dark vignette, clear dark space in
the bottom corners. Cinematic video game promotional art, no watermarks.

If it still refuses: also remove the tagline line (the word PICK next to
LOCKED can contribute) and add the text in an editor afterwards.

--- ORIGINAL DETAILED PROMPT (for less strict generators) ---

Ultra-wide cinematic game key-art banner, photorealistic post-apocalyptic
video game render in the exact visual style of 7 Days To Die. Scene: a
dark, decaying building interior at night - rough wooden walls, cracked
plaster, debris on a worn cobblestone-and-plank floor, cold blue-grey
moonlight leaking through a broken boarded window on the far left.

On the RIGHT THIRD of the frame: a heavy weathered metal security door
with a jail-bar window, rusted rivets and a big old lock plate, set in a
dark doorframe. CENTER-RIGHT, seen from behind at a low three-quarter
angle: a lone survivor in ragged STARTER gear - plain worn t-shirt,
patched cargo trousers, fingerless cloth wraps, no armor - CROUCHED low
on one knee at the door, leaning in, both hands working a thin lockpick
and tension wrench in the lock. Body language is tense and stealthy:
head ducked, weight forward, a small shabby backpack on the back.

The lock is the light source: warm amber-gold light and delicate glowing
sparks spill from the keyhole around the survivor's hands, casting the
only warm glow in an otherwise cold dark scene - same golden particle
energy as magic-light key art. A faint trail of amber particles drifts
off the lock. Deep in the murky background shadows behind the door's
barred window, the barely-visible silhouette of a sleeping zombie -
subtle, easy to miss.

The LEFT THIRD is darker negative space for typography: giant distressed
stencil headline in cracked bone-white paint, all caps:
"SHUT THE FRIGGING DOOR"
and beneath it, smaller, same distressed style:
"SHUT OPEN DOORS. PICK LOCKED ONES. SPARE THE SLEDGE."

Moody, gritty, desaturated brown-and-charcoal palette with one warm
amber accent. Sharp foreground detail, soft dark vignette at the edges.
Leave clear dark space in the bottom-left corner and bottom-right corner
(logos are composited there afterwards). No watermarks, no extra text.
