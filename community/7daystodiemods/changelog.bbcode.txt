[b][size=4]v1.0 - Initial release (July 2026)[/size][/b]

[b]Shut It[/b]
[list]
[*]Doors opened by switches, keys and triggers are properly unlocked - walk through, close them behind you, open them again.
[*]Applies the moment a switch fires, when an area loads, and when you press E on a door that was already stuck open.
[*]Works on existing saves and survives quest resets.
[*]Covers everything that swings, slides or rolls: doors, vault doors, garage doors, roll-ups, hatches, jail doors, drawbridges.
[/list]

[b]Pick It[/b]
[list]
[*]Closed locked doors can be lockpicked - the same hold-to-pick timer as a safe or a police car.
[*]Difficulty scales with the door: wood pops quickly, iron and steel take longer, a vault door will test your patience.
[*]The Lock Picking perk applies exactly as it does to safes: faster picks, fewer breaks.
[*]Progress survives a snapped pick, retries get safer the closer the lock is to giving, and after a few snaps on one door the next attempt is guaranteed. Locks open; they don't farm you.
[/list]

[b]Noise[/b]
[list]
[*]The click of a lock giving way can wake zombies directly behind the door.
[*]A snapping pick rings out - the room and anything nearby may come looking.
[*]Crouch to muffle it; the From The Shadows perk muffles it further, just like your footsteps.
[*]No screamers: lock noise never touches the heat map.
[/list]

[b]What it never touches[/b]
[list]
[*]Player-placed doors: nobody can close, unlock or pick a door you built. Base security is exactly vanilla.
[*]Trader gates: they lock for the night as always and cannot be picked.
[*]Quest objectives: locked quest doors are pickable (same as they were always sledgeable), but objectives are never skipped and quest resets restore every lock.
[/list]

[b]Customising[/b]
[list]
[*]Everything lives in one plain text file (STFDConfig.xml): both feature switches, pick times and break chances per tier, per-door difficulty rules with wildcards, noise loudness, sneak muffling.
[*]Apply edits without restarting: the stfd console command reloads the config and shows what any door resolves to (stfd tier vaultDoor01).
[*]Break the file and the mod falls back to its defaults with a note in the log - an edit can never half-apply.
[/list]

[b]Compatibility[/b]
[list]
[*]Modded doors work automatically - the mod keys off the game's door and lock features, not name lists.
[*]Multiplayer: install on server and clients. Server-only already fixes switch/key doors for every player; lockpicking runs on each player's machine, with the noise sent to the server so zombies genuinely wake.
[*]EAC must be off (like any DLL mod); with EAC on the mod safely skips itself.
[*]Uninstall any time by deleting the mod folder - saves are never touched.
[/list]
